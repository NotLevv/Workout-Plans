Main exercises
1
Pseudo planche push-up
Strength · 4 × 4–6
Rest: 90–120s
Tempo: 3-1-1
Targets: Chest, anterior deltoid, triceps
Cue: Hands beside hips, fingers angled back. Lean shoulders past hands before bending elbows. Keep hips fully extended — don't pike. The further forward you lean, the harder the stimulus.
Overload Increase forward lean by 1–2 cm each week. When lean is maximised, elevate feet (decline pseudo planche).
2
Pike push-up
Strength · 4 × 5–8
Rest: 90s
Tempo: 3-1-1
Targets: Anterior + lateral deltoid, triceps
Cue: Form an inverted V. Hips high, ear-to-ear with arms. Lower until crown of head nearly touches floor. Think "press the floor away from your head".
Overload Elevate feet progressively. Ultimate progression: wall handstand push-up negative.
3
Decline push-up
Hypertrophy · 3 × 10–15
Rest: 75s
Tempo: 2-1-1
Targets: Upper chest, clavicular fibres
Cue: Feet on a chair or couch. Elbows 30–45° from torso — never flared to 90°. Full chest stretch at the bottom. Squeeze at top. Core rigid throughout.
Overload Add reps until 15 clean, then raise surface height by 5–10 cm. Add 1s pause at bottom.
4
Archer push-up
Hypertrophy · 3 × 8–10/side
Rest: 75s
Tempo: 2-0-1
Targets: Unilateral chest, serratus
Cue: Hands wide. Shift weight over one hand and lower to that side — the other arm assists minimally. Bridge toward single-arm push-up. Keep hips square.
Overload Reduce assistance from the straight arm. Progress: elevated archer → full one-arm push-up negatives.
5
Band tricep pushdown
Endurance · 3 × 15–20
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 2-1-2
Targets: Triceps (all heads)
Cue: Anchor band overhead. Upper arms pinned to sides, elbows at 90° start. Extend fully, squeeze hard 1s, controlled return. Slow eccentric is the stimulus.
Overload Add reps to 20, then move hands up the band. Add 1s isometric hold at full extension.
Finisher — push burnout · 4 min
AMRAP 4 minutes — 3 rounds, minimal rest
8 standard push-ups → 6 pike push-ups → 8 shoulder taps (4 per side) → 30s rest. Maintain form — if depth breaks, stop the set. Goal: complete all 3 rounds with quality movement.

Main exercises
1
Pronated-grip pull-up
Strength · 4 × 4–6
Rest: 2 min
Tempo: 2-1-3
Targets: Lats (width), lower traps
Cue: Start from a dead hang. Depress scapulae first, then initiate pull. Drive elbows toward back pockets, not just down. Chin over bar = rep complete. The 3s eccentric is non-negotiable.
Overload Add 1 rep per session. At 3 × 8, add resistance band to waist. Can't do a full rep? Do 5s negatives.
2
Chin-up (supinated grip)
Hypertrophy · 3 × 8–12
Rest: 90s
Tempo: 2-1-2
Targets: Biceps brachii, lats (stretch)
Cue: Palms facing you, shoulder-width. At the top, think "squeezing a pencil in your armpits." Full range — dead hang at bottom, chin clearly above bar at top. No kipping.
Overload Add reps. At 3 × 12, slow eccentric to 4s or add a band loop to waist. Progress toward 3 × 6 weighted.
3
Band bent-over row
Hypertrophy · 4 × 10–15
Rest: 75s
Tempo: 2-1-2
Targets: Rhomboids, mid trap, rear delt
Cue: Stand on band, hinge to ~45°, spine neutral. Row elbows to ribcage — not up toward ears. 1s pause at peak contraction. No rounding the lower back on the return.
Overload Shorten stance on band (more resistance) or use a doubled band. Add 1s squeeze at top. Progress to single-arm.
4
Band face pull
Endurance · 3 × 15–20
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 2-2-2
Targets: Rear delt, external rotators
Cue: Anchor band at forehead height. Pull handles toward ears — hands end beside temples, elbows above shoulders. Externally rotate at peak: "show your armpits." Prioritise control over load.
Overload Increase band tension. Add a 2s hold at peak. Progress to 90° external rotation pause.
5
Band pull-down (kneeling)
Endurance · 3 × 15
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 2-1-2
Targets: Lats, teres major
Cue: Band anchored overhead. Kneel, arms fully extended overhead. Pull elbows down and back to ribcage, keeping chest tall. Think "bend the bar into a U".
Overload Move closer to anchor (more resistance). Single-arm variation challenges anti-rotation.
Finisher — grip & density · 4 min
EMOM 6 minutes (every minute on the minute)
Min 1: max pull-up reps (stop 1 shy of failure). Min 2: 12 band rows (controlled). Min 3: 15 band pull-aparts. Repeat twice. Track total pull-up reps — beat it next session.

Main exercises
1
Bulgarian split squat
Strength · 4 × 6–8/leg
Rest: 2 min
Tempo: 3-1-1
Targets: Quads, glutes, hip flexor stretch
Cue: Rear foot on chair/couch. Front foot far enough that shin stays vertical at the bottom. Lower until rear knee is 1 inch from floor. Drive through front heel. Torso can hinge slightly — this is normal.
Overload Add reps to 10, then increase tempo to 4-2-1. Next: hold a loaded backpack or loop band across front foot.
2
Single-leg Romanian deadlift
Hypertrophy · 3 × 10–12/leg
Rest: 90s
Tempo: 3-1-1
Targets: Hamstrings, glutes, proprioception
Cue: Hinge at the hip — not a squat. Reach trailing leg long behind you as a counterbalance. Spine neutral — imagine a glass of water on your lower back. Deep hamstring stretch at bottom. Squeeze glute hard on return.
Overload Hold a resistance band handle in opposite hand, anchored underfoot. Add 1–2s pause at bottom.
3
Jump squat
Power · 3 × 6–8
Rest: 2 min (full recovery)
Tempo: Explosive — land softly
Targets: Quad, glute power, tendon stiffness
Cue: Descend to quarter-squat, drive explosively. Land toe-heel with soft knees — absorb the impact over 2 full seconds. Never land stiff-legged. Quality > height.
Overload Increase jump height. Progress to single-leg box jump, or add a resistance band loop above knees for eccentric overload on landing.
4
Nordic hamstring curl
Strength · 3 × 4–6
Rest: 2 min
Tempo: 4–6s eccentric
Targets: Hamstrings (max tension)
Cue: Anchor feet under a couch or heavy object. Kneel tall, arms ready to catch. Squeeze glutes, lower your body slowly using hamstrings as a brake. Push off the floor to return. One of the highest-stimulus hamstring exercises in existence.
Overload Slow the eccentric to 6s. When you can lower with control, add a partial concentric phase before the push assist.
5
Shrimp squat progression
Hypertrophy · 3 × 6–10/leg
Rest: 90s
Tempo: 3-1-1
Targets: Quads, single-leg stability
Cue: Hold rear foot behind you (or don't, for assisted version). Lower rear knee toward floor. Front shin vertical. Progress: holding wall → finger assist → unassisted → rear foot elevated.
Overload Reduce wall/finger assist. Add front foot elevation (deficit shrimp). Hold a resistance band loop for additional load.
Finisher — metabolic burnout · 4 min
Tabata — 8 rounds (20s on / 10s off)
Rounds 1–4: Jump squats (or fast BW squats if joints are fatigued). Rounds 5–8: Alternating reverse lunge. Total time: 4 min. Track total reps. Goal: maintain consistent rep count — form degradation = stop the round.

Main exercises
1
Pallof press (band, standing)
Core · 3 × 10–12/side
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 2s out, 2s return
Targets: Anti-rotation core, obliques, QL
Cue: Anchor band at chest height. Stand perpendicular, feet shoulder-width. Tension at sternum. Press directly out, hold 1s, return. The challenge is not rotating toward the anchor. Keep ribs down.
Overload Increase band tension. Wider stance (less base) or try kneeling. Progress: half-kneeling → tall-kneeling Pallof.
2
Single-leg glute bridge
Hypertrophy · 3 × 12–15/leg
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 2-2-2
Targets: Glute max, hip stability, lumbar
Cue: Lie on back, one foot planted. Drive through heel, squeeze glute hard at top — hip fully extended. 2s peak hold every rep. The working hip should not drop. Don't jam into lumbar extension.
Overload Elevate foot on chair. Add band across hips. Progress to single-leg hip thrust.
3
Dead bug
Core · 3 × 8–10/side
Rest: 60s
Tempo: 4s per rep
Targets: Anti-extension core, transverse abdominis
Cue: Low back pressed firmly into the floor — this must not change throughout. Exhale fully, lower opposite arm/leg slowly. The moment your back lifts off the floor, you've lost the rep.
Overload Add band resistance pulling arms toward feet. Slow tempo to 5s. Progress to straight-leg variant.
4
Inverted row (band-assisted)
Hypertrophy · 3 × 12–15
Rest: 75s
Tempo: 2-1-3
Targets: Mid back, rear delt, scapular retractors
Cue: Bar at hip height or sturdy table edge. Body rigid, heels on floor. Pull chest to bar — retract scapulae before bending elbows. Squeeze shoulder blades at top. The 3s eccentric is the priority.
Overload Elevate feet progressively. Add 1s peak hold. Progress toward feet-elevated with weight vest or backpack.
5
Copenhagen plank
Core · 3 × 20–30s/side
Rest: 60s
Tempo: Isometric hold
Targets: Hip adductors, lateral core, glute med
Cue: Side plank with top foot resting on a chair/couch, bottom leg free. Keep hips stacked, do not let them sag or rotate. Breathe normally. Critical for knee and hip health.
Overload Increase hold to 45s. Add hip abduction lifts of the bottom leg. Progress to long-lever (foot on higher surface).
Finisher — core circuit · 5 min
3 rounds, 15s rest between exercises
Plank hold 30s → Side plank 20s per side → Hollow body hold 20s → Rest 30s. Focus on quality contraction, not surviving. If form breaks, stop the hold — reset, continue.

Double Progression Model
The most reliable method for continuous gains without equipment. Follow this hierarchy strictly.
Step 1 — Hit the top of the rep range
Each exercise has a target range (e.g. 3 × 8–12). Only progress when you can complete all sets at the top with clean form and RIR 1–2.
Step 2 — Increase difficulty
Hit the top rep range for 2 consecutive sessions → move to the next harder variation, increase band resistance, slow tempo, or adjust leverage. Reset reps to the bottom of the range.
Step 3 — Track in a log
Write down sets × reps × variation every session. What gets measured gets improved. You cannot manage progression from memory alone. Even a simple notes app is enough.
Deload — every 4th week
Reduce volume by 40% (same exercises, fewer sets). This is not optional — it's when adaptation happens. Skipping deloads is the #1 reason intermediates plateau or get injured.
Tempo as a free tool
Adding a 1–2s pause at the hardest point increases time under tension without changing the exercise. Always exhaust tempo before switching variations.
12-week milestones
Push: pike PU → elevated pike → wall HSPU negative. Pull: 3 pull-ups → 3 × 8 → weighted. Legs: assisted shrimp → unassisted → deficit. Core: plank → RKC plank → ab wheel.
